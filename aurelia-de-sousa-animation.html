"Self-Portrait of Aurélia de Sousa Animation

Explanation

This is an animation made in a workshop environment created as a partnership/initiative of Lusofona University of Porto and Soares dos Reis National Museum. We had to choose a piece of artwork from a list of paintings of various portuguese artists which we had to animate invoking one of the Sustainable Development Goals defined by UNESCO. The piece I chose is a self-portrait made by Aurélia de Sousa on 1882. Nowadays, her figure is deeply connected to feminism because of her indepedence and perseverance on a patriarchal society.

To enhance this symbolic painting and raise awareness about Gender Equality, I selected a poem from a portuguese author, Paula da Graça, one of the first known portuguese feminists. I animated the figure to recite the piece. At the same time I made compositions of different outfits that represent some of the jobs or positions less occupied by women.

This work is set to be displayed on Soares dos Reis National Museum sometime along 2022.
