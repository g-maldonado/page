Paradise Café: A Renascença

A 3D reimagination of a controversial and satiric portuguese game released in 1985. Instead of the original's linear progression we chose a sandbox/exploration approach.

My Job

I was the Co-Producer, Lead Programmer and 3D Artist of Paradise Café: A Renascença.

I did project management and all the programming. I also did some 3D modelling, texturing and populated the game's map.

Due to the ‘’politically incorrect’’ nature and an already established fanbase around the original, the game got some attention and ultimately got us an interview with RetroGames magazine and a showcase on Bienal da Maia 2021.

I used Unity's URP (Universal Rendering Pipeline) and I implemented a renderpass to pixelate the image. I developed a day/night cycle system which drives AI routines and world changes.

The project was developed in 4 months and it's currently on hiatus.

Sinopsis

On Paradise Café: A Renascença you play as Rogério, a poor man who recently lost his mom. After her death, he discovers a connection between a ZX Spectrum game, a bar owner and his family. That place is the Paradise Café and in order to know the truth about his past, he needs enough money to buy the game cartridge. Explore a small town, commit the most vile crimes and get enough money to unbury his mysterious past.
